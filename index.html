The phillipines is a island hopping paradise which is especially famous for its stunning sandy beaches. With over 7,000 islands it can be hard to decide on which ones to visit especially if you are pressed for time. I hope to give you a small glimpse of what this beautiful country has to offer with a backpackers guide of my favourite Island.

Top Picks

Aloha's Grill & Resto Bar

Situated in San Juan this is the perfect place to enjoy some freshly grilled meat/seafood. Simply rock up and choose which meat/seafood you want grilled. Normally served with rice & salad. Prices are reasonable with fresh fish, chicken skewers with a serving of rice & salad costing approx $5.

Cambugahay Falls

A short 30min scooter journey from the San Juan area, this is the largest waterfall on Siquijor Island. To reach the waterfall you will need to pay a small fee to park your scooter and at the entrance with vendors selling snacks/softdrinks/beer. The waterfall has 3 tiers with a huge tarzan rope swing on the bottom tier. The water is very refreshing on a hot humid day!

Cliff Garden Hostel

Very chill location with accommodation built out of bamboo and beanbags outside dorms to relax in the evenings. Situated right on the coast with small cliffs to jump off and go snorkeling & a perfect view of the sunset also to enjoy a beer after your day adventuring.

How to get there...

If you are flying into Manila internationally the quickest route to siquijor is to fly Dumaguette City. This can be as cheap as 700php ($14/£10) excluding baggage depending on avalibility and the flight time is approx 1hr 15mins. From Dumaguette there is a range of ferry options that operate daily. These typically start at 7am which run every 2 hours until late afternoon and cost approx 250php ($5/£3.50). The ferry journey on average takes 50min. Other routes are available depending on the itinerary of your trip such as a ferry from a neighboring island named Bohol.

Top tip

On arrival I would recommend hiring a scooter as this is the most cost effective way of exploring the Island. There is plenty of scooter hire places as you step off the pier and with pro haggling skills this can be as cheap as 250php per day!
